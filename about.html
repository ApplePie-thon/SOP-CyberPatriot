What is CyberPatriot?

CyberPatriot is an exciting, challenging, and competitive program created by the Air & Space Forces Association (AFA) to inspire students toward careers in cybersecurity and other STEM fields. Learn more on the official CyberPatriot website.

Our Club's Mission

Our Computer Club offers students the opportunity to explore the world of cybersecurity through hands-on learning and real team-based competitions. Students can choose subgroups such as CyberPatriot, and start learning key technical skills in systems like Windows, Linux, and networking through Cisco.

Key Features & Workflow

  • Students join the Computer Club and choose subgroups (e.g., Cybersecurity).
  • Students form their own teams from the Cybersecurity subgroup.
  • Each team assigns roles (e.g., Linux Lead, Windows Specialist, Networking).
  • Each system (sign-ups, team creation, resource access) is deadline-based and closes after a set date.
  • Once teams are finalized, private team dashboards allow them to enter/edit system choices, NUCs needed, emails, etc.
  • Team leads and sponsors like Ms. Norris will have backend access for registration and management.

After Team Creation

Once group creation closes, teams will:

  • Access private chat or dashboard to update team preferences and equipment needs.
  • Train in the operating system assigned to their role through the first semester.
  • Communicate with leads and sponsors directly through website contact tools (not Discord).

Resources & Support

  • Training Resources – curated by leads and admins.
  • All presentations and documents will be uploaded and archived yearly.
  • A Help Page will allow students to email or text Mr. Miller directly via AppScript.
  • Scoreboard pages will show real-time results after each round.
  • Previous year’s data will be archived after each season.

Standard Operating Procedures (SOP)

We are building a digital SOP site that includes:

  • Step-by-step documentation for Linux, Windows, Cisco.
  • Checklists and competition preparation guides.
  • Clearly defined team roles and responsibilities.
  • FAQs and support documentation.

Our Goal

Our goal is to build a professional, student-run infrastructure that empowers teams to operate independently, prepares them for competition, and gives both students and sponsors a simple, clean way to manage everything through one platform.
